Water (H2O) is a colorless, tasteless liquid essential for life, made of two hydrogen atoms bonded to one oxygen atom. It’s unique because it can dissolve many substances, moderates Earth’s climate with its high heat capacity, and becomes less dense when it freezes—so ice floats. In living organisms, water transports nutrients, regulates temperature, and enables chemical reactions.

On Earth, it cycles continuously through evaporation, condensation, and precipitation, connecting oceans, atmosphere, and land. Drinking enough water supports cognition, circulation, and kidney function.
